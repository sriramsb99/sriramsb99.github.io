Sriram Bharadwaj

I am a fifth-year PhD student in Theoretical Physics at UCLA, advised by Eric D'Hoker and Leo Zhou. Before beginning my PhD, I received an MSc in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics from the University of Oxford and a BS in Mathematics/Applied Science from UCLA.

My research lies at the intersection of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory, quantum simulation, quantum algorithms, and quantum information theory. I am particularly interested in developing formulations of many-body quantum systems that are both physically faithful and amenable to quantum computation.

More details about my current projects can be found on the Research page, and a list of publications is available on the Publications page.
