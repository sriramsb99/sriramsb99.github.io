Sriram Bharadwaj

PhD Candidate in Theoretical Physics at UCLA

I am a PhD Candidate in Theoretical Physics at UCLA working under the supervision of Eric D'Hoker and Leo Zhou.

My research sits at the intersection of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory and quantum computation. I am broadly interested in reformulating lattice gauge theories in ways that make them more amenable to quantum simulation, and in developing quantum algorithms and simulation methods for classically hard problems. My recent work has included VQE simulations for lattice gauge theory, QAOA-inspired methods for continuous optimization, and adiabatic state preparation. Earlier in my PhD, I worked on theoretical aspects of quantum field theory and string theory.
